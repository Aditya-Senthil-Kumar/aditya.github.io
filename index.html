Projects

FERRARI : The 2015 IPO


This case examines the 2015 IPO of Ferrari by Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA), detailing the strategy to list Ferrari on the NYSE and distribute 90% of its stock publicly. Led by CEO Sergio Marchionne, this move aimed to boost financial health for FCA, provide Ferrari with access to capital markets, and explore luxury goods expansion. Highlighting Ferrari's unique automotive and luxury market positioning, the study underscores the financial and strategic motivations behind the IPO.

THE WRIGLEY RECAPITALIZATION DECISION


The Wrigley case study from Harvard Business School delves into the strategic decision by the Wm. Wrigley Jr. Company, the world's leading chewing gum manufacturer, to undergo a major recapitalization. Facing a declining share price despite strong financial health, the company considered issuing $3 billion in debt for a dividend payout or share repurchase. With a market value of $13.1 billion, no existing debt, and substantial family ownership, the case examines the potential financial impacts, including tax shield benefits, of leveraged recapitalization on Wrigley's value and shareholder returns. This analysis offers insights into the complexities of capital restructuring in enhancing company value.
