FERRARI : The 2015 IPO


This case examines the 2015 IPO of Ferrari by Fiat Chrysler Automobiles (FCA), detailing the strategy to list Ferrari on the NYSE and distribute 90% of its stock publicly. Led by CEO Sergio Marchionne, this move aimed to boost financial health for FCA, provide Ferrari with access to capital markets, and explore luxury goods expansion. Highlighting Ferrari's unique automotive and luxury market positioning, the study underscores the financial and strategic motivations behind the IPO.

THE WRIGLEY RECAPITALIZATION DECISION


The Wrigley case study from Harvard Business School delves into the strategic decision by the Wm. Wrigley Jr. Company, the world's leading chewing gum manufacturer, to undergo a major recapitalization. Facing a declining share price despite strong financial health, the company considered issuing $3 billion in debt for a dividend payout or share repurchase. With a market value of $13.1 billion, no existing debt, and substantial family ownership, the case examines the potential financial impacts, including tax shield benefits, of leveraged recapitalization on Wrigley's value and shareholder returns. This analysis offers insights into the complexities of capital restructuring in enhancing company value.

LOAN ELIGIBILITY PREDICTION


Easy House, a finance company, is revolutionizing its home loan approval process through the power of data analytics. Through this innovation, Easy House seeks to enhance operational efficiency, minimize risks, and concentrate efforts on prospective clients who meet their criteria. The initiative involves the application of three classification models, leveraging the rich dataset to make the loan approval process faster, more accurate, and customer-friendly.

INFOR-LN PATCH UPDATE AUTOMATION


The project involved automating INFOR-LN patch updates through UiPath Orchestrator, SQL, and Python, resulting in enhanced system reliability and a 50% reduction in patch delivery time. It addressed data management issues by implementing SQL solutions to prevent duplicate downloads, improving data integrity by 30% and streamlining processes. Tableau dashboards were developed to monitor the INFOR-LN ERP tool's impact, achieving a 30% decrease in order production time and a 20% boost in on-time deliveries. Additionally, an automated deployment process led to a 90% decrease in patch update time, speeding up software release cycles and enhancing operational efficiency.

INFOR-LN PROCESSES AUTOMATION


The INFOR-LN Process Analysis project aimed at automating manual invoicing and financial processes that handle large sets of data, including ledger accounts and quantities. By incorporating SQL, Python, and RPA tools like UiPath, the initiative automated data extraction, validation, and entry, transforming manual, error-prone tasks into efficient, accurate processes. This automation not only expedited invoicing and financial operations but also improved cross-functional collaboration, supporting business improvements. The outcome was a notable enhancement in productivity and a 40% improvement in response times for key processes, demonstrating the significant benefits of automating complex, data-intensive tasks in the financial domain.

HR-ONBOARDING PROCESS AUTOMATION
